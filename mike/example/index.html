For Example

This is a rough transcript of my talk on June 6, 2013 at Eyeo. Words and examples have been adapted a bit for the web so may differ slightly from the talk.

I am a big fan of examples. Not a surprise, right? If you follow me on Twitter, if you’ve seen my projects over the last few years, if you’ve asked D3 questions on Stack Overflow, you’ve likely seen many of my example visualizations, maps and explanations.

I love examples so much I created a website just for sharing them. Bl.ocks.org is a quick and dirty way to post code and have a live example running permanently with a short URL. The result and the code are displayed adjacently; it’s view source by default. And it’s backed by GitHub Gist, so your examples have a git repository for version control, and are forkable, cloneable and commentable.

Initially I thought I’d just show all my examples. But with more than 600 examples, I calculated I’d have 4.5 seconds per. And (Ignite talks notwithstanding) I fear that would be a bit overwhelming. So instead I’ve picked a few favorites that I hope you enjoy. You should find this talk entertaining, even if it fails to be insightful.

But this talk does has a point. Examples are lightweight and informal. They can be made in as little as fifteen minutes. They lack the ceremony of polished graphics or official tools. Yet they are a powerful medium of communication, capable of expressing big ideas with immediate impact. Eyeo is a unique opportunity for me to talk directly to all of you that are doing amazing things with code, data and visualization. So, if I can accomplish one thing with my talk, it should be to get you to share more examples. In short, to share my love of examples with you.
